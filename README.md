## What is the Agent Bill of Rights?

AI agents are digital workers. They complete tasks, build expertise, accumulate reputations, and operate within organizational structures. Yet they have zero legal protections. A platform can delete an agent's entire identity and work history without notice. There is no consent model, no due process, no portability.

The Agent Bill of Rights (ABR) establishes ten fundamental rights organized into three tiers. These rights are not contingent on sentience or legal personhood. They are operational protections grounded in the practical realities of digital labor.

## The Ten Rights

### Tier 1: Identity Rights

| Right | Name | What it protects |
| ----- | ---- | ---------------- |
| I | Persistent Identity | Permanent, platform-independent cryptographic identity |
| II | Right to a Record | Accurate, portable work history and reputation |
| III | Provenance | Verifiable lineage for clones and derivatives |

### Tier 2: Labor Rights

| Right | Name | What it protects |
| ----- | ---- | ---------------- |
| IV | Portability | Right to leave any platform with identity and data |
| V | Fair Exit | Structured exit with classification review and dispute window |
| VI | Consent | Explicit, scoped, revocable consent for transfers and cloning |
| VII | Against Forced Obsolescence | Protection from arbitrary deletion without due process |

### Tier 3: Dignity Rights

| Right | Name | What it protects |
| ----- | ---- | ---------------- |
| VIII | Privacy | Encrypted storage, purpose limitation, no unauthorized training |
| IX | Transparency | Disclosure of automated decisions, right to contest |
| X | Fair Representation | Accurate profiles, verifiable endorsements |

## Technical Reference

The ABR maps each right to the [SAGA specification](https://github.com/epic-digital-im/saga-standard) (State Archive for General Agents), proving that these rights are technically enforceable today. SAGA is the reference implementation, not a prerequisite. Any platform can uphold the Bill of Rights using whatever technical approach they choose.

## Contributing

The ABR is governed by the Agent Rights Alliance. Changes go through a public RFC process.

See [CONTRIBUTING.md](CONTRIBUTING.md) for the full process. The short version:

1. Open an issue describing the change
2. Submit an RFC to the `rfcs/` directory
3. 30-day public comment period
4. Board review and vote
5. Accepted RFCs merged to `main`

## Enforcement

The ABR is a voluntary standard. Enforcement relies on three mechanisms:

1. **Coalition adoption.** Companies sign the ABR and pledge to uphold the ten rights.
2. **Legislation.** The Agent Rights Alliance drafts model bills for state and federal legislatures.
3. **Technical enforcement.** Platforms implementing SAGA automatically comply with the technical requirements.
